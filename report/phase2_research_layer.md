# Phase 2: Economic Research Layer

This phase adds empirical-discovery tools on top of the Phase 1 nowcast framework. The purpose is to surface economic relationships that can become defensible research findings once the registry is populated with verified live public data.

## Research Questions

1. Which indicators are contemporaneous state activity gauges?
2. Which indicators lead future state GDP growth, and at what horizon?
3. Do states cluster by economic sensitivity, such as labor-market, housing, business-formation, or national-cycle exposure?
4. Do broad deterioration signals anticipate state slowdowns?
5. Do other states' indicators contain spillover information?
6. Which indicators contribute most to bridge nowcasts in each state-quarter?

## Outputs

- `report/research_findings.md`
- `report/lead_lag.csv`
- `report/bridge_sensitivity.csv`
- `report/state_clusters.csv`
- `report/turning_points.csv`
- `report/turning_point_summary.csv`
- `report/spillovers.csv`
- `report/bridge_contributions.csv`

## Interpretation Rule

Synthetic results are method demonstrations only. Live results should be treated as research findings only if they survive registry verification, target-transform robustness checks, and expanding-window evaluation.
